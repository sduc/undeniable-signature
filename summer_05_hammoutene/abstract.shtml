A Demonstrator for the MOVA Undeniable Signatures
 
Author: Malik Hammoutène
 
Supervisors: Prof. Serge Vaudenay
 
Date: Summer Semester 2003-2004
 
Abstract: Undeniable signatures are digital signatures which protect the privacy of the signer: a signer can sign any digital document, and the signature can be verified through an interactive protocol together with the signer. A new undeniable scheme was proposed by EPFL. It makes possible to have very short signatures (typically: 20 to 30 bits). This scheme is called the MOVA scheme (M O stands for M onnerat Jean, and V A for V audenay Serge). Please, read [1] for more information about this protocol. During this 12 weeks internship, I had to program a software in C, imple- menting a variant of this scheme (signer without expert group knowledge). This implementation is a Windows API, using sockets (TCP/IP)..
