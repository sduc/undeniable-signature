Primeless cryptography
 
Author: Sonia Mihaela Bogos
 
Supervisor(s): Ioana Boureanu
 
Date: Summer Semester 2011
 
Abstract:

The goal of this project is to study and construct cryptographic primitives in a primeless manner, i.e. without generating prime numbers.

The motivation for this approach is to reduce the asymptotic complexity of a primitive that is, in many cases, dominated by the generation of prime numbers. For example, the RSA cryptosystem has a complexity of O(l^4) for the setup phase, for generating two different prime numbers p and q. The encryption and decryption processes take only O(l^3). Although we are working with random numbers n, we study the problem of how big should be size of n as we want to maintain the factorization problem hard.
